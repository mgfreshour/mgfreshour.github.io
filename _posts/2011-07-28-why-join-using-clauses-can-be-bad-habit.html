USING clauses. They make straight forward joins look a lot better. The problem is if you get into a habit of using them and doing further join filtering in the WHERE clause. Most of the time, this is perfectly okay because the relational calculus handles things nicely. But what if your SQL becomes more complicated based on certain options? Let's look at an example.
